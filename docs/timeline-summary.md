**Format:**  
Team-based, beginner-friendly, collaborative.

---

# Goals of the Hackathon

1. Encourage women and underrepresented groups in STEM to explore applied, creative problem-solving.
2. Introduce students to real-world research problems and collaborative investigation.
3. Introduce **“vibe coding”**: learning how to prototype and iterate with modern tools such as:
   - VS Code
   - Python
   - Jupyter notebooks
   - GitHub
   - AI copilots
4. Create a **safe and inclusive learning environment** where learning comes first rather than competition.

# Hackathon Presentation Requirements

Each team will work on a project during the hackathon and present their results at the end.

## Presentation Format

Total presentation time: **10 minutes**

- 7 minute presentation
- 3 minute Q&A

Presentations should focus on:

- The **problem**
- The **reasoning process**
- The **insights obtained**

rather than detailed code.

---

# Suggested Presentation Structure

## 1. Problem Description

Explain:

- What problem are you solving?
- Why is it important?
- What motivated the project idea?

---

## 2. Approach and Methodology

Explain:

- How you attempted to solve the problem
- What tools, models, or algorithms were used
- What challenges were encountered

Participants **do not need to show code**, but should clearly explain their reasoning.

---

## 3. Results and Findings

Discuss:

- What the analysis revealed
- Whether the method worked as expected
- Insights or discoveries

---

## 4. Prototype or Demonstration (Optional)

Teams may present:

- A prototype application
- A simple interface or visualization
- A computational or mathematical demonstration
- An interactive notebook

---

## 5. Conclusions and Future Ideas

Discuss:

- What was learned
- How the project could be improved
- Possible future extensions

---

# Code Submission

Each team must provide a **link to their repository**.

The submission may include:

- Jupyter notebooks
- Python scripts
- Data analysis code
- Prototype applications
- Documentation or README files

Code complexity **will not be judged**, but serves as a record of work completed.

---

# Judging Criteria

Projects will be evaluated based on the following.

## 1. Clarity and Quality of Presentation

- Is the problem clearly explained?
- Is it understandable to a general STEM audience?
- Is the presentation engaging?

## 2. Analytical Thinking

- Does the team demonstrate thoughtful reasoning?
- Are the methods appropriate?
- Do the results follow logically?

## 3. Creativity and Innovation

- Is the idea interesting or original?
- Did the team explore creative approaches?

## 4. Implementation and Execution

- Was the idea successfully implemented?
- Does the project include working models or prototypes?

## 5. Impact and Potential

- Could the idea be extended further?
- Does it have potential real-world impact?
